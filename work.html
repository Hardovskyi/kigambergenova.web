Four knots,
one method.

Each project begins as a tangle — a missing public space, a broken urban seam, isolation in the sky, a fieldhouse without a field. I trace the same thread through every one: Node → Context → Strategy → Space → Tectonic → Outcome.

01 Design-Build · Built
Maharashtra, India · 2024

Community Room

A communal pavilion, designed and built by hand in 8 weeks.

The Knot Absence of shared public space at the edge of a village.

Thesis

A simple communal pavilion turns the lack of public space into an open, shaded room for gathering, learning, and everyday use.

Strategy

A roofed enclosure with porous brick walls, pivoting elements, built-in seating, and cross-directional circulation — climatically responsive and flexible.

Outcome

Concept to construction in 8 weeks within a $50k budget. After a site relocation, foundations and the structural grid were replanned in 5 days.

02 Universal Space
Chicago, USA

The Cove

A civic roofscape stitching Upper and Lower Wacker back together.

The Knot Disconnection between urban levels — a residual void breaking pedestrian continuity.

Thesis

A continuous public surface reconnects Upper and Lower Wacker, turning a leftover void into a civic roofscape with a flexible universal space below.

Strategy

Entrances from multiple levels, sun pockets pulling daylight deep inside, and a roof that becomes shared public ground — adaptable across art, market, and fieldhouse programs.

Tectonic

A layered envelope and roofscape act as one system: CLT and concrete structure, a fritted glass skin, radiant systems, and a native planting roof.

  • Level Continuity
  • Daylight Voids
  • Open Interior
  • Wind & Shadow Analysis
  • CLT + Concrete
SiteWacker Drive, Chicago
TypeCivic / Universal Space
FocusUrban Connector
AnalysisSun · Wind · Daylight
03 Bangkok Studio
Vertical Urbanism

Village in the City

An intergenerational tower with social streets in the sky.

The Knot Social isolation in vertical living — high-rise life weakening generational ties.

Thesis

A vertical village reconnecting park, city, and community through shared terraces, collective programs, and social streets in the sky.

Strategy

Distinct units for students, elders, and families woven together by shared floors — wellness, learning, marketplace, childcare — that force daily contact.

Tectonic

Terraces and voids tested as environmental devices: wind studies distribute airflow for shaded, comfortable communal space in Bangkok’s hot, humid climate.

  • Students · Elderly · Families
  • Shared Terraces
  • Wind Distribution
  • Lumphini Park Edge
  • Climate-Responsive
CityBangkok
ProgramMixed-use Tower
ThemeIntergenerational Living
ClimateHot · Humid
04 Fieldhouse without a Field
Neighborhood Civic

The Wave

A neighborhood fieldhouse organized as a gradient of public life.

The Knot Neighborhood life lives in thresholds — porches, sidewalks, stairs, small moments of contact.

Thesis

A fieldhouse organized as a gradient of public life — open civic gathering above, descending to quieter, intimate wellness spaces below.

Strategy

Threshold culture becomes a layered section: the exterior stair turns into a public room as interiors shift from bright social programs to calm, low-light recovery.

Space

Bright & public, active & semi-public, dark & intimate — three registers of light choreographing how the building is used floor by floor.

  • Bright · Public
  • Active · Support
  • Dark · Calm
  • Experiential Section
  • Stair as Public Room
TypeNeighborhood Fieldhouse
IdeaLight to Dark Gradient
LevelsBasement → Roof
DriverThreshold Culture
